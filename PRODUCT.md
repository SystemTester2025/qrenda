# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Small business owners, freelancers, entrepreneurs, and professionals who need modern digital business cards. They land on this page looking for a smarter alternative to paper cards — they want something shareable, trackable, and impressive. They're mobile-first, time-pressed, and judge within seconds.

## Product Purpose

Qrenda is a SaaS platform for creating, sharing, and managing digital business cards (vcards). The homepage exists to convert visitors into signups — communicating the product's value instantly, establishing trust, and removing friction from the decision to try it.

## Brand Personality

Confident, crisp, warm, and direct. Not playful, not corporate — precise and human. Speaks like an expert who doesn't need to shout.  

Three words: **Composed. Sharp. Warm.**

## Anti-references

- No SaaS cliché template (identical card grids, gradient text, hero-metric numbers)
- No "editorial-typographic" magazine aesthetic (italic serifs, ruled separators, monospace metadata)
- No beige/sand/warm-neutral body backgrounds
- No tiny uppercase tracked labels above every section (eyebrow labels)
- No glassmorphism or decorative blur
- No side-stripe borders on cards
- No numbered section markers (01, 02, 03) as decorative scaffolding
- No gradient text with background-clip

## Design Principles

1. **Confidence through restraint** — Fewer elements, more space, bigger typography. Every section earns its place. If it doesn't convert or clarify, remove it.
2. **One decisive visual move per section** — No hedging. A dark hero is dark. A white section is white. Alternating contrast creates rhythm.
3. **Typography as the primary texture** — Hierarchy through weight and scale, not decoration. Generous sizes, tight tracking on display text, comfortable line-height on body.
4. **Motion with purpose** — Reveal animations that orient, not dazzle. No fade-on-scroll for every block; choreograph the hero load, then let scroll reveals feel earned.
5. **Mobile-first, edge-to-edge** — The homepage works perfectly on a phone first. Desktop gets more breathing room, not more content.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG 2.1 AA minimum (4.5:1 body text, 3:1 large text)
- Reduced motion respected via `prefers-reduced-motion`
- Focus states visible on all interactive elements
- Form labels associated correctly
- Color not used as the only differentiator
